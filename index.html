(Body Mass Index)

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a ratio calculated from weight and height. The BMI is a reliable indicator of body fatness for most children and teens.

(HbA1c)

The HbA1c test is presently a preferred test of doctors to check whether diabetes is under control. This is important as blood glucose levels fluctuate from minute to minute, hour to hour, and day to day.

(Tests to stay healthy with Diabetes)

Diet, Exercise, Medicine and monitoring are the 4 pillars for management of diabetes. To know if one is effectively managing diabetes, one needs the help of monitoring.

Monitoring

Monitoring is an integral part of diabetes treatment, as this can help you tell wherher your isulin, diet or exercise plan are working or not. This is determined by the blood glucose either reading high or low.

Changing Diabetes® in Children

Our Vision

To initiate and strive for comprehensive diabetes care for the economically underprivileged children with T1DM below 18 years.
Enhancing Healthcare by facilitating awareness and education/empower stakeholders with knowledge about the state of diabetes care.

Making a difference through commitment, compassion and care for children with diabetes.

Our Mission & Goals

"Making a difference through commitment, compassion and care for children with diabetes"

Diabetes Awareness

To sensitize key stakeholders and strengthen the attention required by children having type 1 diabetes focused towards creating solution towards diagnosis, diabetes management and social acceptance.

Diabetes Awareness

Capacity building -Training & Education

To enhance the capabilities of health care professionals which includes both primary care physicians and other paramedical professionals in diagnosis & treatment of children with type 1 diabetes.

Capacity building -Training & Education

Patient Education camps and tools

To increase awareness on self-management among children & their family as diabetes management is 24x7 and there is replacement to diabetes education. In fact for best results it’s essential that the children learn to manage themselves at the earliest.

Patient Education camps and tools

Infrastructure

To strengthen the capacity of the health care system in the cities involved in the programme to diagnose and treat diabetes in children.

Infrastructure
